## Morning ‚Üí Afternoon ‚Äî [Fix]

**What:** Multi-iteration fix on the calculus comparison panel's LaTeX formulas. Tried three approaches before landing:

1. **First attempt (inline base64, 200 DPI):** 4 of 6 images failed to render ‚Äî only NSA column worked. Character-mangling in long inline strings suspected.
2. **Second attempt (GitHub raw URLs via `createImageAsync`):** Pushed PNGs to the public repo's `assets/` folder, fetched them via URL. Images didn't render either ‚Äî Figma's sandbox appears to restrict or fail silently on URL fetches from the `use_figma` tool path.
3. **Third attempt (inline base64, 150 DPI):** All 6 loaded but thin glyphs (`lim`, `‚à´`, `Œî`) were chopped into fragments. Root cause: my transparency conversion made pixels with luminance > 200 fully transparent, which ate the anti-aliased edges of thin strokes.
4. **Final (inline base64, 200 DPI, alpha-preserving transparency, split into 2 calls):** Changed the Python transparency pass from a threshold approach to `alpha = 255 - luminance`, which preserves anti-aliasing gradients properly. Split the upload into two `use_figma` calls (derivative row + integral row) to isolate any per-call failures. All 6 rendered cleanly.
**Why:** The calculus comparison is one of the paper's four intuitive wins; having it broken on the presentation canvas undermined the "calculus, simpler" claim.
**Means:** Two technical findings worth remembering for future Figma canvas work ‚Äî (1) prefer alpha-gradient transparency over a cutoff threshold for PNG text/math, (2) split large image uploads into multiple smaller `use_figma` calls for reliability.
